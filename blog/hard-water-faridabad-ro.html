How Hard Water in Faridabad Affects Your RO Water Purifier

Faridabad is known for its high ground-water hardness. If you live in areas like Greenfield, Surajkund, or Neharpar, your RO system is fighting a tough battle every day.

The ground water in Faridabad often reaches TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) levels of 1500 to 2500 PPM. In some industrial sectors, it even crosses 3000 PPM. While an RO (Reverse Osmosis) system is designed to handle this, the sheer density of minerals like Calcium and Magnesium in our local water puts immense strain on your machine.

1. Scaling of the RO Membrane

The membrane is the "heart" of your purifier. When hard water passes through it, the high concentration of minerals starts forming a white, chalky layer (scale) on the membrane surface. This scaling chokes the microscopic pores, leading to:

  • Reduced water flow (it takes longer to fill the tank).
  • Increased rejection water (more waste water).
  • Change in taste as the membrane fails to filter properly.

2. Frequent Filter Choking

Before reaching the membrane, water passes through the Pre-filter, Sediment filter, and Pre-Carbon filter. In Faridabad, these filters often turn dark brown or black within 3-4 months due to heavy sediment and iron content. If not replaced, this pressure can damage the booster pump.

3. Impact on the Booster Pump

When the membrane or filters are partially choked, the booster pump has to work much harder to push water through. This leads to the pump overheating and eventually failing, which is one of the most expensive repairs in an RO system.

Maintenance Tips for Faridabad Residents

To ensure your family always has access to pure drinking water, we recommend the following:

01

External Pre-filter Change: Replace the outer "candle" filter every 3 months. It costs very little but saves your expensive internal filters.

02

TDS Monitoring: Check your output water TDS monthly. It should ideally be between 50 and 150 PPM.

03

Anti-Scalant Balls: Ask your technician to add anti-scalant balls in the pre-filter to reduce scaling on the membrane.
