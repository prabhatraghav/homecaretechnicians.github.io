Is Your Kitchen Chimney a Fire Trap ?
The Hidden Danger of Grease Buildup

Indian cooking involves high-flame sautéing and deep frying. Without a clean chimney, your kitchen might be hosting a silent, highly flammable threat.

Most homeowners in Faridabad view their kitchen chimney only as a tool to remove smoke and smell. However, the primary job of a chimney is to trap grease and oil particles. Over time, this grease accumulates inside the filters, the internal housing, and the motor.

1. The "Fuel" Inside Your Filters

Grease is highly flammable. When the mesh or baffle filters of your chimney are saturated with old, dried oil, they become a layer of fuel. If a high flame from your gas stove reaches the chimney, or if there is a "flash fire" while frying, the grease can ignite instantly.

2. Blocked Suction and Motor Overheating

When filters are choked, the chimney motor has to work much harder to pull air. This causes the motor to vibrate excessively and overheat. An overheated motor, combined with the presence of oil nearby, is a recipe for an electrical fire that can spread through the ducting.

3. Dripping Oil Hazard

Have you noticed oil dripping from the corners of your chimney or onto your gas hob? This is a clear sign that the internal oil collector and housing are full. This dripping oil can fall onto a hot burner, causing a sudden flare-up that is difficult to control.

Is your chimney making more noise than usual?

Noise and poor suction are the first warning signs of grease overload. Don't risk a kitchen fire.
