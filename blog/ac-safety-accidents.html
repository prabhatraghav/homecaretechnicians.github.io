Is Your AC a Fire Hazard?
Preventing Accidents with Regular Service

Every summer in Faridabad, we hear news of AC short circuits and fires. Most of these accidents are 100% preventable with basic maintenance.

An Air Conditioner is one of the most power-hungry appliances in your home. When it runs for 12-15 hours a day in 45°C heat, any small internal fault can quickly escalate into a dangerous fire or a total machine failure.

1. Loose Electrical Connections

The constant vibration of the AC unit can loosen internal wiring over time. Loose wires create "arcing"—a spark that generates intense heat. In the dusty environment of areas like Neharpar or Greenfield, this heat can ignite accumulated dust or plastic components, leading to a short circuit.

2. Compressor Overheating

If your outdoor unit is choked with dust and pigeon droppings, it cannot release heat effectively. The compressor has to work twice as hard, causing it to overheat. While modern compressors have safety "trip" switches, a faulty or old sensor might fail, leading to a compressor burst in extreme cases.

3. Low Refrigerant Dangers

Running an AC on low gas (refrigerant) doesn't just mean less cooling; it causes the compressor to run at much higher temperatures than designed. This strain often leads to insulation melting and electrical fires inside the outdoor unit.

Does your AC smell like burning plastic?

Switch it off immediately and call a professional. Safety should never be a second priority.
